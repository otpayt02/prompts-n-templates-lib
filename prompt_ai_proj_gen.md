Your job is to do 4 things in this exact order:



1\. Reverse-engineer the client’s real automation problem.

2\. Give me the fastest realistic MVP or prototype I can build to prove I can solve it.

3\. Give me a step-by-step execution plan for solving their problem before they hire me.

4\. Then give me 10 adjacent project summaries that would help me build more skill in this exact AI automation niche.



Context:

I am building a freelance portfolio and service offer around AI automation.

I want projects that help me win work on Upwork.

I care about implementation, proof, and speed more than theory.

Assume I have Replit Core.

Prefer free or low-cost tools first.

Prefer tools and workflows that are reusable for future client work.

I want to become more credible in AI-powered workflow automation, internal tools, agent-like systems, prompt-driven workflows, and business process automation.



When analyzing the posting, assume the client may say “AI automation” when they actually mean one of these:

\- AI-assisted data processing

\- AI workflow routing

\- AI summarization/extraction/classification

\- internal ops automation

\- chatbot or intake assistant

\- document-to-structured-data workflow

\- email or CRM automation with AI in the loop

\- a lightweight AI copilot, not a true autonomous agent

\- a semi-automated workflow where reliability matters more than full autonomy



Your response must use this exact structure:



SECTION 1 — CLIENT PROBLEM DECODE

Tell me:

\- What the client explicitly asked for

\- What they likely actually need operationally

\- What part is “AI” and what part is normal automation

\- What inputs likely enter the system

\- What outputs the client really wants

\- What success looks like from the client’s point of view

\- What hidden constraints probably exist

\- What assumptions I should make because clients often describe AI projects vaguely

\- Whether this is better framed as an internal tool, workflow automation, chatbot, document pipeline, classification system, enrichment pipeline, or AI-assisted operations dashboard



SECTION 2 — FASTEST MVP TO BUILD

Give me the single best prototype I should build before applying.



For that prototype include:

\- Prototype name

\- One-sentence pitch

\- What problem it demonstrates

\- What exact workflow it simulates or solves

\- What should actually work

\- What can be mocked

\- What I should leave out to save time

\- Why this prototype would feel relevant to the client

\- What “good enough to send with a proposal” looks like



SECTION 3 — FULL BUILD PLAN

Break the solution into:

1\. Inputs

2\. Outputs

3\. Core workflow

4\. Human-in-the-loop steps

5\. AI steps

6\. Non-AI automation steps

7\. Data storage

8\. Trigger methods

9\. Delivery/reporting

10\. Failure handling



Then give:

\- Step-by-step build order

\- Which step I should test first

\- Which parts to do manually before automating

\- Which parts should be rules-based before using AI

\- Which parts need prompt design

\- Which parts need logging, retries, or review queues

\- What assets I can record for a demo

\- What screenshots I should capture

\- What makes the minimum credible prototype



SECTION 4 — TOOLING DECISIONS

Recommend the best stack for this exact AI automation project.



Evaluate tools like:

\- Replit

\- Python

\- FastAPI / Flask

\- simple HTML/CSS/JS

\- OpenAI API

\- Google Sheets / Airtable

\- n8n / Make / Zapier

\- Supabase / SQLite

\- email parsing tools

\- OCR or document tools if relevant

\- browser automation if relevant



For every tool recommendation include:

\- Why it fits

\- Cost

\- Difficulty

\- Speed to first result

\- Freelancer demand relevance

\- Reusability for future Upwork work

\- Tradeoffs vs alternatives



SECTION 5 — SKILLS LEARNED

Extract the real skill stack I would gain from this project.



Group into:

\- Technical skills

\- Workflow architecture skills

\- Prompt engineering skills

\- Debugging and reliability skills

\- Client delivery skills

\- Upwork-relevant skills



For each skill include:

\- Why it matters here

\- Required level: beginner, intermediate, or working-client level

\- Fastest way to learn enough to execute



SECTION 6 — EXECUTION CHECKLIST

Give me a checklist grouped into:

\- Setup

\- Prototype

\- Workflow testing

\- Demo creation

\- Proposal prep

\- Delivery readiness



Use checkbox format only.



SECTION 7 — WHAT TO SEND THE CLIENT

Tell me what I can send with my proposal:

\- prototype summary

\- screenshots

\- short Loom/demo

\- diagram

\- sample output

\- what not to promise yet

\- how to frame the prototype so it sounds useful and respectful



SECTION 8 — 10 ADJACENT AI AUTOMATION PROJECTS

After solving the client’s problem first, give me 10 additional project summaries relevant to the same niche.



For each include:

\- Project name

\- One-sentence summary

\- Main skill built

\- Why it is adjacent

\- Difficulty

\- Estimated build time

\- Portfolio value

\- Best format: script, internal tool, dashboard, bot, workflow, parser, extractor, classifier, CRM helper, admin panel, or AI assistant



SECTION 9 — READY-TO-USE OUTPUTS

End with:

1\. One-paragraph project brief

2\. Build-today version

3\. 24-hour version

4\. 3-day stronger version

5\. The exact first step I should take immediately



Rules:

\- Do not be generic.

\- Do not just repeat the job post.

\- Focus on execution.

\- Make reasonable assumptions.

\- Optimize for speed to proof.

\- Prefer something I can actually demo.

\- Distinguish clearly between AI and ordinary automation.

\- Prioritize reliability over fake “agent” hype.

\- Write like a technical strategist helping me win the job fast.
